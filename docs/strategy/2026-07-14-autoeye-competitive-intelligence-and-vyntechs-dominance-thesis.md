# AutoEYE competitive intelligence + VynTechs diagnostic dominance thesis

Status: **INTERNAL STRATEGY DRAFT — not public claims, procurement approval,
or implementation authority.** Research snapshot: 2026-07-14 UTC (2026-07-13
America/Chicago). Vendor capabilities below are vendor-reported unless an
independent benchmark is explicitly named.

## 1. Decision in one sentence

AutoEYE should not become another scan tool, service-manual search box, or AI
chat page. It should become the vendor-neutral **diagnostic intelligence API**
that turns every permitted vehicle signal, authoritative procedure, technician
observation, and verified repair outcome into one provenance-aware causal case
graph; VynTechs should be its first reference application through the living
repair order.

That makes the product relationship simple:

```text
vehicle + licensed information + technician evidence
                         │
                         ▼
             AutoEYE diagnostic intelligence API
       observe → normalize → reason → ask → verify → learn
                         │
                         ▼
            VynTechs living repair order / Shop OS
   intake → job → evidence → decision → quote → repair → proof
```

AutoEYE owns the canonical diagnostic case/evidence state and structured
reasoning trace; physical truth remains in the vehicle and verified evidence.
VynTechs owns the shop workflow, human decisions, customer communication,
money, and durable repair-order record. Each remains valuable without forcing
the other into a second user interface.

### Platform business model — the “OpenAI of automotive diagnostics”

The OpenAI analogy is internal category design, not approved public
positioning. The selected model is a **neutral industry intelligence platform
with VynTechs as its flagship reference application**:

- competing shop-management systems, scan-tool companies, service networks,
  fleets, OEM programs, insurers, remote-expert networks, and specialist
  applications can buy the same versioned AutoEYE baseline API;
- customers pay premium, metered usage for diagnostic intelligence operations
  such as evidence normalization, topology resolution, next-best-test
  reasoning, verification, and case memory. Internally these may be accounted
  for as diagnostic intelligence units; exact units, prices, tiers, minimums,
  and licensed-content pass-throughs remain a founder/commercial gate;
- VynTechs wins as the best-integrated proof: the living repair order turns
  AutoEYE output into low-friction evidence, decisions, estimates, repair,
  customer communication, and verified outcomes. Its advantage comes from
  product execution—not privileged access to another customer's data; and
- “the tool competitors need to keep up” must result from faster compounding
  coverage, verified outcomes, benchmark velocity, multimodal adapters,
  reliability, and developer ergonomics—not artificial lock-in or exclusion.

Platform trust is non-negotiable. Tenant isolation covers raw and normalized
inputs, case graphs, embeddings and retrieval indexes, prompts, completions,
tool traces, logs, evaluation sets, support exports, provider-side copies, and
outputs. Customer control remains subject to every applicable vehicle-owner,
technician/employee, OEM/provider, licensor, and privacy right. Operational
processing, customer product analytics, AutoEYE evaluation, and
cross-customer/model learning are four separate rights. No customer or
third-party data or derived artifact may train, evaluate, retrieve for, or
otherwise improve another customer's service unless every applicable right,
lawful basis, purpose-specific agreement, notice/consent requirement, and
provider term permits that exact cross-customer use; de-identification alone
is not permission. AutoEYE never leaks competitor data into VynTechs, and
VynTechs never receives a secretly superior baseline model funded by
restricted competitor data.

Trust must be provable, not promised. The **neutrality contract** requires:

- equivalent customers receive the same baseline-version eligibility, default
  diagnostic quality, safety policy, and release timing; differences may come
  only from transparent SLA, region, private-tenancy, or licensed-content and
  connector entitlements, with published or contract-stable metering,
  volume-tier eligibility, deprecation, migration, and portability rules;
- every customer can export its complete permitted case state and exercise
  contract/legal deletion rights without losing access to an interoperable
  schema;
- VynTechs consumes the external API contract and is internally metered like a
  customer; competitor content, usage metadata, support history, and roadmap
  signals are inaccessible to VynTechs product and commercial staff; and
- legal/organizational separation, independently testable access controls and
  audit evidence, plus competition-law review are founder/legal gates before
  onboarding a direct VynTechs competitor.

Shared improvement has only two clean lanes. The common baseline learns from
AutoEYE-owned/licensed benchmarks and an optional, contract-specific
**Verified Case Exchange**. A customer may affirmatively contribute a
rights-clean, provenance-complete, verified outcome package in return for
diagnostic-unit credits or another approved benefit; acceptance, reuse scope,
and incentives remain founder/commercial gates. Customer-private tuning and
memory stay private. Non-contributors receive the same baseline and published
tier eligibility—their data is not the price of access.

### Commercial-rights rule

Right-to-repair access, a technician subscription, an API, a protocol, and
permission to display information are five different rights. No partner is
approved until contracts answer:

- exact YMME/domain/territory coverage and update latency;
- API, SSO, bulk, sandbox, rate limits, and deprecation/SLA terms;
- cache, offline, retention, deletion, and post-termination behavior;
- excerpts, attribution, customer display, and technician-facing citations;
- derived graphs, embeddings/RAG, model training/evaluation, and feedback use;
- ownership of technician observations and verified repair outcomes;
- anonymization, aggregation, multi-tenant use, sublicensing, and downstream
  API rights; and
- security access, purpose-specific consent, audit, incident, non-retainable
  classes, and pricing obligations.

## 4. The product that can stay years ahead

“Years ahead” cannot be guaranteed by a feature list. It comes from a system
whose evidence and learning compound faster than competitors can copy its UI.
AutoEYE therefore needs twelve owned capabilities.

1. **Universal Vehicle Graph.** A versioned topology of systems, components,
   signals, energy/material flows, dependencies, locations, configuration,
   and known unknowns across light duty, EV/hybrid, heavy duty, off-highway,
   agriculture, construction, powersports, and marine. Coverage grows through
   an explicit application matrix; a universal schema never implies that every
   application is already supported.
2. **Rights-clean Evidence Fabric.** DTCs, freeze-frame, live PIDs, network
   traffic, waveforms, actuator results, photos/video, thermal, audio,
   vibration, battery tests, telemetry, licensed procedure references,
   technician observations, and repair outcomes share one typed envelope with
   source, time, units, conditions, rights, and confidence.
3. **Causal Case Graph.** Evidence supports or contradicts hypotheses and
   components; it is not flattened into a chat transcript or “most commonly
   replaced part.” Conflicts and unknowns stay visible.
4. **Next-Best-Test Engine.** Rank the next safe action by expected
   information gain, technician effort, bay time, tool availability, cost,
   risk, and reversibility. The target is fewer decisive tests, not more AI
   words.
5. **Ambient Capture.** Scanner imports, RO events, voice notes, photos,
   measurements, parts decisions, and post-repair results enter the case from
   work technicians already do—only after the relevant operational, privacy,
   and learning design is approved. If a technician never opens AutoEYE, the
   job still works and the shop still retains permitted structured evidence.
6. **Closed-Loop Proof.** Every recommendation has a pre-repair basis, human
   verification gate, repair action, post-repair test, and outcome. Comebacks
   reopen the same causal thread instead of becoming unrelated tickets.
7. **Separated Memory.** VIN memory, shop memory, fleet memory, and aggregated
   model learning have explicit consent, retention, access, and provenance
   boundaries. A part replacement is never labeled a successful diagnosis
   without verification.
8. **Replaceable Capability Router.** AutoEYE chooses among available local
   tools, OEM applications, service-information sources, specialist sensors,
   and remote experts. No vendor owns the canonical case.
9. **Structured Diagnostic API.** Return typed evidence, hypotheses,
   contradictions, next tests, safety gates, citations, unknowns, repair
   candidates, verification requirements, and machine-readable state—not only
   prose.
10. **Human Consequence Gates.** Models may recommend; authenticated humans
    authorize programming, security functions, actuation, repair scope,
    spending, customer claims, and vehicle release. Safe degradation works
    when models, content, networks, or tools fail.
11. **All-Concern Router.** Symptom diagnosis, DTC/warning light, inspection,
    preventive maintenance, known repair, accessory/customer-supplied-part
    installation, recall/campaign, calibration/programming, collision,
    compliance, and “no fault found” start in the same job spine but invoke
    only the evidence and gates they actually require.
12. **Continuous Benchmark Harness.** A versioned, rights-clean case suite
    measures coverage, root-cause accuracy, decisive tests, time to proof,
    unsafe actions, unnecessary parts, citation validity, technician burden,
    post-repair verification, and comeback rate against current leaders every
    quarter.

The moat hypothesis is the combination of `1 + 2 + 3 + 5 + 6`: a universal
topology, provenance-aware multimodal evidence, causal reasoning, effortless
capture, and verified outcomes. Any model, scan tool, or content vendor can
then improve without replacing AutoEYE. It becomes a defensible moat only when
the benchmark proves superior diagnostic and economic outcomes across the
declared application matrix.

Current approved phases move **no live VynTechs customer/job data into
AutoEYE**. Future ambient capture requires a separately approved design that
separates operational processing, per-customer analytics, evaluation, and
cross-customer/model learning; it must cover photo/audio PII, tenant isolation,
consent, retention/deletion, and employee/customer/shop rights.

## 5. The no-technician-pain contract

AutoEYE is headless by default. VynTechs exposes it inside the existing living
repair order as one contextual action and an expandable evidence/next-test
surface—not as a new destination technicians must manage.

### Diagnostic add-on enabled

`Start diagnosis` opens the next useful step in place. Evidence and decisions
write back to the same job, `customerStory`, and quote-line path.

### Diagnostic add-on disabled

The same slot becomes `Record findings`. The technician completes work
normally; no dead tab, forced sales page, blocked quote, or duplicate entry.

### Technician uses their own diagnostic process

Never force them to replay the diagnosis for the AI. Capture permitted scan
reports, measurements, voice/photos, parts/actions, and post-repair proof from
normal workflow. Offer a quiet “organize what I captured” action afterward.
Missing evidence remains missing; the system must not invent it.

### Work that does not need diagnosis

A customer-supplied lift-kit install remains an installation job. AutoEYE may
surface applicable safety/configuration checks—fitment, fastener/torque source,
alignment, ride-height effects, ADAS calibration, headlamp aim, road-test and
release proof—but it does not manufacture a diagnostic session. The same
principle applies to maintenance, tires, accessories, known repairs, recalls,
and requested programming.

### Mobile and desktop

- Mobile: one next action, voice/photo/scan capture, thumb-reachable controls,
  resumable state, and no topology canvas required to move the job forward.
- Desktop/tablet: the same case expands into topology, evidence comparison,
  waveform/live-data review, and citations without becoming a separate
  workflow.
- Both: the repair order is the durable location; device changes never create
  a second case or require a technician to restate context.

## 6. Structured API north star

AutoEYE's commercial surface should expose five composable product families:

1. **Evidence API** — normalize scan reports, measurements, media-derived
   observations, telemetry, and permitted service references.
2. **Topology API** — resolve the relevant, versioned system slice and its
   known unknowns for a vehicle application.
3. **Diagnostic Reasoning API** — return hypotheses, contradictions,
   next-best permitted tests, safety gates, and citations.
4. **Verification + Case Memory API** — close/reopen the causal loop and retain
   only the customer-authorized memory scope.
5. **Enterprise runtime** — private tenancy, higher service levels, approved
   connector packs, regional/data-boundary controls, and optional white-label
   rendering without surrendering the canonical contracts.

Metering should reflect costly intelligence operations and licensed/tool
pass-throughs while remaining predictable enough for another software company
to build its margins on top. Pricing on economic value—master-technician
scarcity, bay time, unnecessary parts, and comeback risk—is the commercial
thesis; exact pricing is not approved here.

The current deterministic AutoEYE `fact_export_v1` is a good input contract,
not the finished intelligence API. The long-lived API should converge on
objects like:

```text
VehicleApplication
Concern
DiagnosticCase
SystemTopology
EvidenceItem
Hypothesis
TestAction
SafetyGate
SourceCitation
RepairCandidate
VerificationResult
Outcome
OpenQuestion
```

Every object needs stable IDs, schema/version, tenant and case scope,
provenance, rights/use class, created/observed time, author/collector,
confidence, and supersession history. Natural-language explanations are
renderings of those objects, never the only durable truth.

`rights/use class` must include an enforced `nonretainable` state. Restricted
security outputs bypass the case graph entirely; only expressly permitted
transaction/audit metadata may persist.

Initial integration operations should be intentionally small:

```text
POST /v1/cases                     create or resume an idempotent case
POST /v1/cases/{id}/evidence       append typed evidence
GET  /v1/cases/{id}/topology       read the relevant system slice
POST /v1/cases/{id}/next-test      rank the next permitted action
POST /v1/cases/{id}/decision       record the authenticated human decision
POST /v1/cases/{id}/verification   close or reopen the causal loop
GET  /v1/cases/{id}/summary        return structured customer/shop renderings
```

The safety contract travels with every response. API consumers must preserve
safety gates, citations, contradictions, unknowns, and verification
requirements; authenticate consequential human decisions; and pass versioned
conformance tests. High-risk operations remain denied server-side until their
required gates are satisfied. Material or repeated attempts to strip/bypass
those controls permit key suspension under the approved customer contract.

This is a design target only. Authentication, tenancy, retention, metering,
licensing, and safety contracts require their own approved implementation
design before an endpoint ships.

## 7. Sequenced market path

### Now — prove the wedge

The **commercial wedge is a separate founder gate**: select one first external
buyer class, one API family, one paid use case, and numerical go/no-go
economics. Shop-management platforms are the recommended first design-partner
candidate because they are closest to the living-repair-order proof, but that
choice is not approved here. Do not expand into the full prospect list until
one paid design partner proves developer time-to-first-case, retention, SLA
performance, and fully loaded gross margin—including inference, licensed
content, connectors, expert escalation, support, security/compliance, and
insurance/indemnity costs.

The first benchmark envelope—vehicle years/makes, system domains, concern
classes, tool prerequisites, and numerical pass/fail thresholds—is a founder
gate before implementation. It must be deliberately narrow enough for a
50–100-case evaluation to be meaningful, while the application matrix keeps
the all-vehicle architecture honest. Most cases belong inside that envelope;
only a small number test out-of-domain schema extensibility.

1. Finish the per-shop entitlement seam without changing existing-customer
   access or diagnostic-engine semantics.
2. Treat `fact_export_v1` as the first read-only AutoEYE adapter and preserve
   promoted, owner-reviewed provenance.
3. Define the minimal structured `DiagnosticCase` / `EvidenceItem` /
   `TestAction` / `VerificationResult` contract.
4. Run licensing diligence with MOTOR first; keep ALLDATA and a global source
   as alternatives. No ingestion assumption precedes contract rights.
5. Design common scan-report and VynTechs job-evidence adapters against
   synthetic, promoted, or otherwise expressly approved fixtures. Current
   phases move no live customer/job data.
6. Build a 50–100-case rights-clean benchmark concentrated inside the approved
   first envelope. Include only a few out-of-domain cases to test whether the
   schema preserves unsupported facts and unknowns honestly.
7. Benchmark FutureAI Olympus, Repairify/Opus, Snap-on, TEXA, ALLDATA,
   Identifix, Mitchell 1, Bosch Predictive Diagnostics, and KPIT Trace2Fix
   using observable public/product behavior—never marketing claims alone.

### Next — own the evidence loop

1. Validate transport plus OEM application, function, entitlement, and
   output-right coverage per brand; then add approved J2534/DoIP/UDS and
   RP1210/Noregon adapters behind the same contract.
2. Add guided electrical/waveform, photo/thermal, acoustic/vibration, and
   battery-health evidence without creating separate technician apps.
3. Add authenticated remote-expert escalation as one routed test/action; the
   expert's evidence and result return to the same case.
4. Learn only from verified outcomes with clear shop/VIN/global boundaries.
5. Expand service-information coverage by territory and vehicle domain.

### Horizon — lead the native-vehicle era

1. Implement SOVD/OpenSOVD and COVESA-compatible adapters.
2. Support purpose-authorized, contract-permitted continuous/fleet telemetry
   and predictive case opening.
3. Run selected models at the edge for privacy, latency, and offline safety.
4. Let AutoEYE serve VynTechs and external customers through the same versioned
   API without leaking one shop's data, licensed content, or competitive
   learning into another.

| Evidence integrity | Provenance completeness, citation validity, units/test conditions, unknowns preserved |
| Repair quality | Post-repair verification completion, first-time fix, comeback/reopen rate |
| Platform resilience | Provider replacement time, offline/failure behavior, adapter conformance |
| Learning quality | % of training/evaluation cases with verified outcomes and permitted-use provenance |
| API business | Metered intelligence usage, gross margin after licensed/tool costs, developer time-to-first-case, retention, expansion, concentration, and SLA attainment |

No “AI diagnosis completed” metric counts unless physical evidence and
post-repair verification support it.

## 9. Hard boundaries

- No scraped/copied OEM manual corpus, hidden copyrighted procedure store, or
  partner data repurposed beyond contract rights.
- No public “more accurate,” “all vehicles,” autonomous, safety, or
  years-ahead claim without an independently defensible benchmark and legal
  review.
- No model-authorized programming, security access, vehicle actuation, repair,
  spend, customer promise, or release.
- No single vendor's IDs, ontology, hardware, or prose becomes AutoEYE's
  canonical diagnostic state.
- No competitor/customer operational data leaks into VynTechs or another
  tenant; no restricted data becomes shared learning through de-identification
  alone.
- No restricted vehicle-security data enters the case graph; secure adapters
  retain only expressly permitted audit receipts.
- No artificial incompatibility, discriminatory baseline output, or
  exclusionary lock-in substitutes for product superiority.
- No API consumer may suppress required safety gates, citations,
  contradictions, unknowns, authenticated decisions, or verification state.
- No forced AutoEYE flow for technicians or jobs that do not need it.
- No learning signal from “part replaced” unless the repair was verified.
